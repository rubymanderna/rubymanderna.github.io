Research Experience

Graduate Research Assistant – Computational Quantum Chemistry 2022 – Present

Foley Lab, UNC Charlotte · Advisor: Dr. Jonathan J. Foley IV

  • Ab initio cavity QED method development: Derived and implemented paramertized and self-consistent field (SCF) procedures for cavity QED within the electronic strong-coupling regime (cQED-RHF), extending Hartree–Fock and CCSD frameworks to polaritonic systems; benchmarked against full-CI references.
  • Quantum algorithm design: Constructed second quantized Hamiltonians for strongly coupled light–matter systems.
  • Open quantum systems & noise modeling: Implemented Lindblad/GKSL master equation formalism to capture photon loss and decoherence; computed Purcell enhancement factors and dyadic Green's tensor couplings for plasmonic nano-cavities.
  • Computational benchmarking: Systematically evaluated accuracy/cost trade-offs of parameterized vs. self-consistent cQED approaches, analogous to active-space selection and quantum resource estimation.
  • Open-source software: Core developer of the MD-CQED Python package (rubymanderna/MD-CQED); contributed to SCF driver, two-electron integral routines, Fock/density matrix, and Löwdin orthogonalization (NumPy/SciPy + Psi4).

Mehata Lab, Delhi Technological University · Advisor: Dr. Mohan Singh Mehata

  • Synthesized AgNPs via plant-extract-mediated green routes; characterized surface plasmon resonance via UV-Vis/NIR spectroscopy (Lambda 750, 190–32,000 nm).
